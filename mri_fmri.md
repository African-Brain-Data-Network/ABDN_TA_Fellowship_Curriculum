# MRI / fMRI Curriculum (ABDN TA)

## Overview
Welcome to the ABDN MRI Cohort fellowship programme. This document outlines the course objectives, curriculum, and assessment structure for the training program.

---

## Course Objectives

By the end of this training program, students should be able to:

- Identify research gaps and draft a comprehensive data analysis plan.
- Understand the basics of MRI physics, including different MRI sequences.
- Identify different brain structures in both healthy individuals and pathological cases (e.g., stroke, Multiple Sclerosis, Alzheimer's Disease).
- Preprocess functional MRI (fMRI) data successfully using FSL.

---

## Module 1 — Foundations of MRI and MRI Physics
**Learning Objective:** Understand MRI physics and brain anatomy  

- Week 1: Basics of Brain Anatomy  
- Week 2: What is MRI?, MRI Sequences
- Week 3: Magnetic Fields & Signals  
- Week 4: Image Formation  

---

## Module 2 — Overview of MRI
**Learning Objective:** Understand how MRI/fMRI data is collected  

- Week 1: MRI Sequences (T1, T2)  
- Week 2: fMRI Basics (BOLD signal)  
- Week 3: Experimental Design  
- Week 4: Safety and Ethics  

---

## Module 3 — Data Analysis
**Learning Objective:** Understand and learn structural and functional MRI data processing  

- Week 1: Intro to fMRI tools  
- Week 2: SPM and FSL  
- Week 3: Preprocessing pipelines  
- Week 4: Statistical Analysis  

---

## Module 4 — Neurology in Diseases

- Week 1: Neuroimaging in Disease  
- Week 2: Cognitive Neuroscience Studies  
- Week 3: Stroke
- Week 4: Alzheimer's Disease

---

## Student Assessments and Deliverables 

- Monthly quizzes to assess theortical knowledge
- Weekly deliverables for consistent practical skill building
- Draft a data analysis plan
- Individual capstone project
- Drafting, revisions, and presentation
